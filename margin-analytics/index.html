How to Read This Table — Margins, Op Cost & Maturity explained
Realized Margin
(Revenue – Already Paid) ÷ Revenue

“If payouts stopped today, what's left?” Good for mature cohorts, misleading for young ones because they haven't paid most traders yet.
📌 Example: A 2026 cohort shows 90%+ realized margin but it's only ~10% mature. Most payouts haven't happened yet.
Projected Margin ★ Most Important
(Revenue – Paid – IBNR) ÷ Revenue

The true estimated final gross margin once all traders have been paid. Uses the historical payout curve to estimate future costs.
📌 Target: keep above 20%. Below 10% = alarm. Negative = losing money on that cohort.
🏦 Net Profit Margin
(Revenue – Projected Cost – OpEx) ÷ Revenue

Bottom-line profit after all trader payouts and operating costs. Adjust the OpEx % control to model different cost scenarios.
📌 Default: 25% OpEx. Net Margin = Projected Margin minus OpEx assumption.
Maturity
% of expected lifetime payouts already made

Shows how reliable the Projected Margin estimate is:
70%+ Mature → very reliable
40–70% Maturing → moderate
<40% Young/New → many ahead
📌 63.15% = lifetime payout rate. A “Mature” cohort has paid everything the curve predicts.

Fixed Operational Cost % of Revenue / month — adjusts Op Cost & Net Profit Margin live

How Future Projections Work — What are M+0, M+1, M+2?
What is M+1, M+2, M+3?
When you sell a challenge, that month = M+0. Then:
M+1 = 1 month after sale
M+2 = 2 months after sale
M+3 = 3 months after sale
...up to M+24 (2 years)

The payout curve tells us what % of a cohort's total lifetime payouts happen at each M.
Example: M+1 = 19.6% of revenue. M+2 = 17.1%. First 3 months = 48.7% of all lifetime payouts.
How to read the projection chart
In each future calendar month, multiple cohorts are all at different M-offsets simultaneously:

For each cohort C in future month F:
lag = F − C.month
contribution = C.revenue × curve[lag]

Sum all cohorts = total expected payout for that month.
This is purely from existing cohorts. New sales are not included.
Why does it drop fast?
Most payouts happen early (M+1 = 19.6%, M+2 = 17.1%). By M+4 you're already down to 4.3%.

So as time passes, existing cohorts “age out” of their peak payout months and the tail becomes small.

The big driver is recent, large cohorts. When they age past M+3, obligations drop sharply.
By M+3 you've paid ~48.7% of ALL lifetime payouts for a cohort.

📉 How to read the Payout Curve
The payout curve was built from mature cohorts (2023-12 onwards). It shows: for every $100 of revenue collected in month 0, how much do you expect to pay traders at each future month?

Monthly chart (bars): shows the % paid in EACH individual month. M+1 is the biggest (19.6%) — traders who pass their phase in month 1 get funded and start getting paid.
Cumulative chart (line): shows the running total. By M+3 you've already paid ~48.7% of lifetime total. By M+12 you've paid ~62.1%. Full 24-month run = 63.15%.

🔑 Key insight: The first 3 months after sale = 48.7% of ALL future payouts. Your most recent cohort's sales will produce most of their cost in the next 3 months.
